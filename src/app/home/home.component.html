                                        Day 2: Upper Body Strength Training

                                        and tricep dips. Rest for 60 to 90 seconds between sets.

                                        Day 3: Circuit Training and Plyometrics
                                        Design a challenging circuit that combines strength exercises and explosive
                                        plyometric movements. Perform 3 rounds of the circuit, with minimal rest between
                                        exercises and rounds. Include exercises like jump squats, box jumps, kettlebell
                                        swings, push-ups, lunges, and planks.

                                        Day 4: Active Recovery and Cardio
                                        Engage in a low-impact activity for 60 minutes, such as swimming, cycling, or

                                        Focus on compound exercises that target the major muscle groups of the lower

                                        Begin with a 10-minute dynamic warm-up. Perform 5 rounds of HIIT, alternating
                                        between 30 seconds of all-out effort (sprints, cycling, rowing, etc.) and 30

                                        resistance training session focusing on compound exercises like squats,
                                        deadlifts, bench press, overhead press, pull-ups, and rows. Perform 5 sets of
                                        5-8 repetitions with heavy weights, aiming to increase the weights
                                        progressively.

                                        Day 2: Rest and Recovery
                                        Take a complete rest day to allow your body to recover and repair.

                                        Day 3: Plyometric Training and Upper Body Hypertrophy
                                        Engage in explosive plyometric exercises such as box jumps, depth jumps, and
                                        medicine ball throws for 20-30 minutes. Follow this with an upper body
                                        hypertrophy workout, targeting individual muscle groups with exercises like
                                        bench press, incline dumbbell press, bent-over rows, pull-ups, shoulder press,
                                        and bicep curls. Perform 3-4 sets of 8-12 repetitions with challenging weights.

                                        Day 4: Active Recovery and Low-Intensity Cardio
                                        Engage in low-impact activities such as swimming, cycling, or yoga for 60
                                        minutes to promote active recovery and improve flexibility and mobility.

                                        Day 5: Lower Body Hypertrophy and Core Training
                                        Focus on lower body hypertrophy exercises like squats, lunges, Romanian
                                        deadlifts, hip thrusts, and calf raises. Perform 3-4 sets of 8-12 repetitions

                                        exercises like planks, Russian twists, hanging leg raises, and cable
                                        woodchoppers. Aim for 3-4 sets of 10-15 repetitions for each exercise.
